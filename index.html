Our History

Beginnings

The roots of St. Mary’s Jacobite Syrian Church, Naigaon – Vasai trace back to St. Thomas Syrian Church, Byculla, Mumbai, founded in 1975. Families began settling in Mira Road, Bhayandar, Naigaon, Vasai, Nallasopara, and Virar, and were initially dependent on Byculla and Malad congregations for Holy Qurbana.

Foundation

  • 29 Nov 1987 – First Holy Qurbana celebrated by Rev. Fr. Varghese Valayil.
  • Dec 1998 – Vasai declared independent service center; Rev. Fr. Kuriakose Kadavumbhagam appointed first vicar.
  • Land Donation – 65-cent plot at Naigaon East donated by Mr. Antony John Pereira, through Chev. K. V. John.

Growth of the Parish

  • 13 Sep 1999 – Foundation stone consecrated by H. G. Thomas Mor Themotheos.
  • 10 Apr 2000 – Altar stone blessed by H. H. Moran Mor Ignatius Zakka I, Patriarch of Antioch.
  • 8–9 Jun 2002 – Holy Mooron Consecration of the new church by Metropolitans.
  • Sep 2002 – Fr. Abraham Padanilathu Memorial Library inaugurated with 5000+ books, thanks to Chev. C. M. Joy.

Holy Soonoro

Our church is privileged to house the Holy Soonoro of Mother Mary, a sacred relic that blesses the congregation and inspires devotion. Intercessory prayers are conducted every Wednesday, strengthening the faith of the parish community.

📷 Holy Soonoro

Present Day

Today, St. Mary’s Church has over 100 families. Holy Qurbana is held every Sunday at Naigaon and at Nallasopara. The parish also runs:

  • Sunday School for children
  • Regular prayer meetings
  • Parish Youth Association & Martha Mariam Vanitha Samajam
  • Our own cemetery – unique among Jacobite Churches in Mumbai

Vision for the Future

With the grace of God, our parish aims to expand its charitable mission by establishing:

  • A charitable hospital
  • A creche
  • A vocational training center for women
